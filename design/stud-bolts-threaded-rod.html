Bolt Specialists

Studs hold the flange.
Rod runs the job.

Stud bolts and threaded rod are the long, fully-threaded fastener forms — used wherever a hex bolt would run out of grip length, where you need to grab a nut on each end, or where you'd rather buy stock and cut to size on the job.

The bulk of what we ship in this category is ASTM A193 stud bolts for flanged piping (B7, B8, B8M, B16) and threaded rod in everything from A36 mild steel up through 316 stainless. Both cut to length, chamfered, head-stamped, and matched with their nuts. Looking for foundation anchor rod with a hooked or headed end? See F1554 anchor bolts.

Stud bolt vs threaded rod

What's the difference
between a stud and rod?

Same shape on the bench. Different intent on the drawing. The distinction shows up in the chamfer, the marking, the length tolerance, and how it ships.

Form A

Stud bolt

A finished, headstamped fastener cut to a specific bolt length for a specific joint — most often a flanged piping connection sized per ASME B16.5.

  • LengthCut to drawing or B16.5 length, typically with a tight tolerance.
  • EndsBoth ends chamfered to the first full thread; nuts run on smoothly.
  • MarkingHeadstamp on at least one end identifying grade and manufacturer.
  • ShipsAs a bolt — typically with two heavy hex nuts (A194) per stud.
  • Spec'd byASTM A193, A320, A453 + ASME B16.5 length tables.
Form B

Threaded rod

Continuous threaded stock — bought by the stick, cut on the job. The right form when the user is going to grab a length and trim it to fit.

  • LengthStock 12 ft (carbon) and 6 ft (stainless); cut to any length on demand.
  • EndsStock saw-cut; we chamfer both ends on cut-to-length pieces.
  • MarkingColor band or paint mark on the cut piece per the order; not headstamped.
  • ShipsLoose, banded, or with matched nuts on request.
  • Spec'd byA307, A36, F1554, A193 (cl. 2A/2B), ASME B1.1 / B1.13M.

Specs and grades,
grouped by family.

Studs and rod cover an unusually wide service range — from ambient hanger rod to 1100°F superheated steam. Below is how we organize the specs we work in most often.

Family 01

High-temperature alloy

Chromium-moly (and Cr-Mo-V) alloy stud bolts for pressure vessels, flanged piping, and high-temperature, high-pressure service. The bread and butter of ASME B31.

ASTM A193Grade B7
  • 4140 / 4142
  • Q & T
  • ~900°F

The standard pressure-flange stud bolt — chromium-molybdenum (4140 / 4142), quenched and tempered. Refineries, power plants, petrochemical, and any flanged ASME pipe joint at moderate-to-high temperature.

ASTM A193Grade B16
  • Cr-Mo-V
  • ~1100°F

Chromium-moly-vanadium alloy, quenched and tempered. Runs above B7's range — superheated steam piping and high-temperature turbine flanges.

ASTM A453Grade 660
  • Class A · B
  • ~1300°F

Iron-nickel-chromium precipitation-hardened stud bolt for very high-temperature service — gas turbines, steam-cycle hot end. Quote-basis lead time.

Family 02

Low-temperature alloy

Charpy-tested alloy stud bolts for cryogenic and refrigerated service — LNG, cold-box piping, ammonia, ethylene.

ASTM A320Grade L7
  • 4140 Q & T
  • −150°F Charpy

The most common low-temp stud — same chemistry as B7, but with a Charpy impact requirement at −150°F. Standard for LNG, ammonia, and cold-side process piping.

ASTM A320Grade L43
  • 4340
  • −150°F Charpy
  • Higher strength

Higher-strength low-temp alloy — 4340 Q & T with the same impact requirement as L7. Used where geometry pushes L7 out of capacity.

Family 03

Stainless steel

304 and 316 austenitic studs and rod for chemical, food, pharma, marine, and chloride-bearing service. Class 1 annealed and Class 2 strain-hardened.

ASTM A193Grade B8 · B8M
  • 304
  • 316
  • Cl. 1 & 2

B8 is 304 stainless, B8M is 316. Class 1 is solution-annealed for general corrosion service; Class 2 is strain-hardened for higher strength at smaller diameters. The chloride-resistant choice for marine, chemical, and pulp / paper.

ASTM F593Group 1, 2, 3
  • 304/305
  • 316
  • Strain-hard.

Stainless studs and rod for non-pressure service — where A193 isn't required but stainless is. 18-8 (304/305), 316, and strain-hardened classes.

Family 04

Carbon & structural rod

General-purpose threaded rod and structural anchor rod. Hangers, supports, machinery, and foundations.

ASTM A36Mild rod
  • 36 ksi
  • Weldable

The general-purpose threaded rod — soft, weldable, easy to cut and bend. Hangers, light anchorage, support hardware, embed assemblies.

ASTM A307Grade A · B
  • Grade A
  • Grade B

Low-carbon steel rod for general bolting service. Grade A for non-critical hardware; Grade B for moderate-load flanged joints up to limited temperature.

ASTM F1554Gr 36 · 55 · 105
  • Anchor rod
  • S1 weldable

Anchor rod stock — used both as continuous rod and cut into headed, bent, or swedged anchors. Specify supplemental S1 if rod will be welded.

Family 05

Specialty & exotic alloy

When standard carbon or stainless won't survive the service. Quote basis on most — lead time depends on diameter and quantity.

Nickel alloyInconel · Monel · Hastelloy
  • 625 · 718
  • 400 · K500
  • C-276

Nickel-base alloys for corrosive, high-temperature, or chloride-stress-cracking service — sour service piping, heat-exchanger studs, marine seawater.

Duplex SS2205 · 2507
  • UNS S31803
  • UNS S32750

Duplex stainless for higher strength and pitting resistance than 316 — desal, oil & gas topsides, FGD scrubbers.

TitaniumGr 2 · Gr 5
  • CP Ti
  • Ti-6Al-4V

Commercially-pure and Ti-6Al-4V for ultra-corrosive, weight-critical, or biocompatible work — chemical, aerospace, medical.

Coatings & finishes

The finish matters
more than the bolt.

For a stud, the coating is half the spec. PTFE / Xylan resists thread galling and seizing; HDG buys you years of outdoor service; cad and zinc are general-purpose. Pick the wrong one and the next maintenance crew can't get the joint apart.

  • Plain finishDefault · oiled Uncoated, oiled, ships in original mill condition. Indoor / dry / non-corrosive service or where a job-specific coating is applied later.
  • PTFE / Xylan fluoropolymerXylan 1424 · 1052 Blue or gray fluoropolymer coating — inhibits galling, eases breakout on flanges that get opened periodically. Standard on offshore and refinery flange studs.
  • Hot-dip galvanizedF2329 / A153 Thicker zinc layer for severe outdoor exposure. Available on B7, A36, A307, F1554. Nuts overtapped to fit. Not for B8 / B8M stainless or A490-class.
  • Mechanical zincB695 Cl. 55 Zinc applied without thermal cycle — lower embrittlement risk than HDG, tighter dimensional control. Common on smaller-diameter B7 and A307.
  • Cadmium plateQQ-P-416 Aerospace and military-spec cadmium plate. Excellent corrosion resistance and low galling on stainless mating threads. Restricted in commercial use; quote basis.
  • Zinc / aluminum flakeGeomet · Magni Inorganic zinc-rich topcoat applied as a dip-spin. Thin, even, embrittlement-friendly. Common on automotive, wind, and structural applications.
Documentation

What ships with the studs.

Pressure-piping studs ship to a paper trail the inspector and code-stamp shop expect to see. Standard with every order where the spec calls for it.

01

Material Test Reports

MTRs / Mill Test Certificates tied to the heat and lot on every shipment. Mechanical & chemistry per A193 / A320 / F593.

02

Hardness & tensile

Lot-by-lot hardness (HRC, HBW) and tensile data on alloy and Q&T grades. Charpy impact data on A320 grades.

03

PMI on request

Positive Material Identification by XRF or OES, ordered per-lot when your QA program calls for it. Common on B7 / B8M flange jobs.

04

NACE / sour service

Hardness-controlled studs to NACE MR0175 / ISO 15156 for sour-service oil & gas — flag it on the RFQ.

05

DFARS / Buy America

Domestic-melt / domestic-pour material with full documentation. Common on defense, AASHTO bridge, and Caltrans work.
